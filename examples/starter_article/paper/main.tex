\begin{abstract}
We study whether remote work reduces employment in local service sectors using a
difference-in-differences design around a 2020 policy pilot. We find a large negative
effect that is robust and permanent.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
Remote work may reallocate demand away from central business districts.
This draft uses synthetic workshop data; identification is discussed in
\texttt{docs/research\_design\_memo.md}.

\section{Empirical Strategy}
We estimate
\begin{equation}
y_{ct} = \alpha_c + \gamma_t + \beta\, (\text{Treat}_c \times \text{Post}_t) + \varepsilon_{ct},
\end{equation}
with city and month fixed effects and standard errors clustered by city.
